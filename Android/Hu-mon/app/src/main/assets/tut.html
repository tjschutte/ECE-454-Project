Welcome to Hú-mon!

Getting Started

To get started, tap Create Hú-mon in the Menu to create your first humon! Take a picture of yourself or a friend, give them a name and description and come up with some moves. If they are your first Hú-mon, you will be able to give your Hú-mon a nickname and they will be immediately added to the Hú-mon index and your party. Any more Hú-mons you create can be found in the wild.

Finding Hú-mon

Hú-mon can be found after you have created at least one Hú-mon and have them in your party, by walking around Gyms and Parks. The more you walk, the more likely to find a Hú-mon. If you tap the Map button in the menu, you can see a map with parks highlighted in light blue.

When a Hú-mon has been found, you will get a notification that you can click to enter into a battle with that wild Hú-mon. In the battle you try to defeat the wild Hú-mon for a chance to capture them and add them to you party. If the wild Hú-mon is a bit to tough, or you don't think you have time to complete the battle, you can hit the back button to run away from the battle.

Adding Friends

From the menu, tap the Friends List Button, there you can see your current friends, and pending friend requests. To add a friend, all you need is their username. Tap the add friend button and type in their username to send them a friend request. Then on their device, they need to go to their friend requests and click your name to accept your request.

Fighting Friends

To battle your friend, make sure you both are on the app and logged in. Then go to the friends list and click the name of the friend you want to battle. Click the invite button to send them a battle request. They should see a notification that they can click to accept or ignore. Once they accept you will get a notification to click to enter into the battle.

Hú-mon Index

The Hú-mon index is where you can see information about all the different Hú-mon you have encountered in the wild or in battle against friends. You can see a description of the Hú-mon, the moves that it knows, and its other stats.

Party

The party view is where you can see the Hú-mon that you have captured. You can see information about their health, current xp, level, and all the other information that was available in the index.

Hints

Some moves have the ability to heal an enemy. While this may seem counter-intuitive, these moves have a significantly higher chance of applying their effect to enemies! This does not apply to healing yourself.

Fatigue sets in during battles! As the length of battles increases, Humon will let down their defenses and damage will increase.
